# On the Influence of Carbonic Acid in the Air upon the Temperature of the Ground

**Source**: [On the Influence of Carbonic Acid in the Air upon the Temperature of the Ground](../raw/On%20the%20Influence%20of%20Carbonic%20Acid%20in%20the%20Air%20upon%20the%20Temperature%20of%20the%20Ground.md)  
**Date**: [1896-04]  
**Type**: Historical document, scientific paper  
**Nature**: Historical  
**Author**: [[Svante Arrhenius]]

## Summary

This landmark 1896 paper by Swedish physicist [[Svante Arrhenius]] presents the first quantitative calculation of how changes in atmospheric carbon dioxide (carbonic acid) affect Earth's temperature. Arrhenius calculated that doubling CO₂ would raise global temperatures by 5-6°C, and proposed that variations in atmospheric CO₂ could explain ice ages and warm periods in Earth's history.

### Key Scientific Contributions

**Quantitative Climate Modeling**
- First systematic calculation of greenhouse effect
- Used [[Langley]]'s moon radiation measurements to determine atmospheric absorption
- Calculated absorption coefficients for CO₂ and water vapor across spectrum
- Developed mathematical framework relating CO₂ concentration to temperature

**Major Findings**
- Increasing CO₂ from 1.0 to 1.5 times present value → ~3.5°C warming
- Decreasing CO₂ to 0.67 times present value → ~3°C cooling
- Effect increases with latitude (greater at poles than equator)
- Effect greater in winter than summer
- Logarithmic relationship: temperature increases arithmetically as CO₂ increases geometrically

**Ice Age Theory**
- Proposed CO₂ variations as explanation for glacial/interglacial cycles
- Calculated that CO₂ at 0.62-0.55 of present value would produce ice age conditions (4-5°C cooling)
- Calculated that CO₂ at 2.5-3 times present value would produce Tertiary warm period (8-9°C warming)
- Rejected astronomical theories (Croll's hypothesis) as inadequate

### Methodology

**Data Sources**
- [[Samuel Langley]]'s measurements of moon radiation through atmosphere
- Analyzed 24 series of observations at different atmospheric conditions
- Calculated absorption by CO₂ and H₂O for 21 different wavelength groups
- Used Stefan's law of radiation for heat transfer calculations

**Innovative Techniques**
- Corrected Langley's data for temporal variations in atmospheric clarity
- Calculated "mean path" of Earth's radiation through atmosphere (factor of ~1.6)
- Accounted for different absorption at different altitudes
- Included effects of clouds, ocean vs. land, snow cover
- Calculated seasonal and latitudinal variations

**Key Parameters**
- K = quantity of carbonic acid (K=1 for present atmosphere)
- W = quantity of water vapor
- β = absorption coefficient for Earth's radiation
- α = absorption coefficient for solar radiation (~0.40)
- Calculated β ≈ 0.70-0.79 for typical atmospheric conditions

### Atmospheric Physics Insights

**Selective Absorption**
- CO₂ and H₂O absorb strongly in infrared (Earth's radiation)
- Minimal absorption of visible light (solar radiation)
- Absorption occurs in specific bands, not continuously
- CO₂ absorption bands at λ = 2.6μ, 4.6μ, and longer wavelengths
- H₂O absorption bands throughout infrared spectrum

**Radiative Transfer**
- Upper atmosphere (15,000m) radiates to space at ~-42°C
- Lower atmosphere (233m) absorbs Earth's radiation
- Temperature difference between layers crucial for greenhouse effect
- Clouds at different heights have different effects

**Feedback Mechanisms**
- Warming increases water vapor, amplifying CO₂ effect
- Snow/ice albedo changes create secondary effects
- Ocean currents redistribute heat
- Nebulosity (cloudiness) moderates effects

### Geological Applications

**Explaining Climate History**
- Tertiary warm period: CO₂ at 2.5-3× present → 8-9°C warmer
- Ice ages: CO₂ at 0.55-0.62× present → 4-5°C cooler
- Interglacial periods: CO₂ near present levels
- Simultaneous global climate changes explained

**CO₂ Sources and Sinks** (from [[Högbom]])
- Volcanic exhalations: primary source
- Weathering of silicates: primary sink
- Coal burning (1896): ~1/1000 of atmospheric CO₂ per year
- Limestone formation: enormous quantities over geological time
- Ocean absorption/release: regulator of atmospheric CO₂

**Volcanic Activity**
- Periods of intense volcanism → high CO₂ → warm climates
- Periods of volcanic quiescence → low CO₂ → ice ages
- Not continuous decline, but oscillations over geological time

## Historical Significance

**Scientific Firsts**
- First quantitative calculation of greenhouse effect
- First proposal of CO₂-driven ice ages
- First climate model with mathematical framework
- First recognition of logarithmic CO₂-temperature relationship
- First calculation of climate sensitivity

**Prescience**
- Recognized industrial CO₂ emissions could affect climate
- Understood feedback mechanisms (water vapor, albedo)
- Recognized global nature of CO₂-driven climate change
- Anticipated modern climate science by decades

**Limitations (by modern standards)**
- Underestimated climate sensitivity slightly
- Limited data on atmospheric composition
- Simplified treatment of clouds and ocean
- No consideration of other greenhouse gases
- Assumed CO₂ changes would be slow (geological timescales)

## Mathematical Framework

**Basic Equation**
T⁴ = K / (1 + ν(1 - β))

Where:
- T = absolute temperature
- K = constant (solar input + heat transport)
- ν = albedo parameter
- β = absorption coefficient

**Climate Sensitivity**
- ΔT ≈ 5-6°C for doubling CO₂
- ΔT ≈ 3°C for halving CO₂
- Logarithmic relationship: ΔT ∝ log(CO₂)

**Latitudinal Variation**
- Equator: ~3°C per doubling
- 40-50°N: ~3.5°C per doubling
- 60-70°N: ~3.5-4°C per doubling
- Polar amplification from snow/ice feedback
