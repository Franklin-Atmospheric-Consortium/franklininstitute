# Svante Arrhenius

**Source**: [Svante Arrhenius](../raw/Svante%20Arrhenius.md)  
**Date**: [1859-1927]  
**Type**: Historical document, biographical article  
**Nature**: Historical  
**Subject**: [[Svante August Arrhenius]], Swedish scientist

## Summary

Biographical article about [[Svante August Arrhenius]] (1859-1927), Swedish physicist and chemist who founded physical chemistry, won the 1903 Nobel Prize in Chemistry, and was the first scientist to calculate how atmospheric CO₂ affects Earth's temperature. His 1896 work on the greenhouse effect laid the foundation for modern climate science.

### Life and Career

**Early Years**
- Born February 19, 1859, near Uppsala, Sweden
- Child prodigy: taught himself to read at age 3
- Arithmetical prodigy by watching father's account books
- Entered cathedral school at age 8, starting in fifth grade
- Graduated 1876 as youngest and most able student

**Education and Early Research**
- Studied at Uppsala University
- Dissatisfied with instructors, moved to Stockholm (1881)
- Studied under physicist [[Erik Edlund]] at Physical Institute
- 1884: Submitted 150-page dissertation on electrolytic conductivity
- Received only third-class degree (professors unimpressed)
- Same work later earned him 1903 Nobel Prize

**Major Scientific Contributions**

1. **Ionic Dissociation Theory** (1884)
   - Explained that salts dissociate into charged particles (ions) when dissolved
   - Contradicted prevailing belief that ions only formed during electrolysis
   - Proposed chemical reactions in solution were reactions between ions
   - Foundation of modern physical chemistry

2. **Arrhenius Equation** (1889)
   - Developed concept of activation energy
   - Quantified relationship between activation energy and reaction rate
   - Still fundamental to chemistry today

3. **Acid-Base Theory** (1884)
   - Defined acids as substances producing hydrogen ions
   - Defined bases as substances producing hydroxide ions
   - "Arrhenius acids" and "Arrhenius bases" named after him

4. **Greenhouse Effect Calculations** (1896)
   - First quantitative calculation of CO₂'s effect on temperature
   - Predicted doubling CO₂ would raise temperature ~4°C
   - Proposed CO₂ variations explain ice ages
   - See [[On the Influence of Carbonic Acid in the Air upon the Temperature of the Ground]]

**Career Progression**
- 1891: Lecturer at Stockholm University College
- 1895: Professor of physics (with opposition)
- 1896: Rector
- 1901: Elected to Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences
- 1903: Nobel Prize in Chemistry (first Swedish laureate)
- 1905-1927: Director of Nobel Institute for Physical Research
- 1911: First Willard Gibbs Award

### Greenhouse Effect Work

**Arrhenius's Rule** (1896)
> "If the quantity of carbonic acid increases in geometric progression, the augmentation of the temperature will increase nearly in arithmetic progression."

Modern formulation: ΔF = α ln(C/C₀)
- Logarithmic relationship between CO₂ and temperature
- α (alpha) for CO₂ = 5.35 W/m² for Earth's atmosphere

**Key Findings**
- Reducing CO₂ to 0.62-0.55 of present → 4-5°C cooling (ice age)
- Increasing CO₂ to 2.5-3 times present → 8-9°C warming (Tertiary climate)
- First to predict industrial emissions could cause global warming
- Included water vapor feedback and latitudinal effects
- Omitted clouds, convection, and other factors

**Data Sources**
- Used infrared observations of moon by [[Frank Washington Very]] and [[Samuel Pierpont Langley]]
- Calculated IR absorption by CO₂ and H₂O
- Applied Stefan-Boltzmann law

**Reception and Legacy**
- Criticized by [[Knut Ångström]] (1900) - claimed CO₂ absorption "saturated"
- Arrhenius replied strongly (1901)
- Work largely ignored for decades
- Vindicated by [[Charles David Keeling]] in 1960s
- Now recognized as foundation of climate science

**Popular Writing** (*Worlds in the Making*, 1908)
Arrhenius viewed CO₂ increase optimistically:
> "We may hope to enjoy ages with more equable and better climates, especially as regards the colder regions of the earth, ages when the earth will bring forth much more abundant crops than at present, for the benefit of rapidly propagating mankind."

He saw industrial CO₂ as preventing future ice ages and benefiting agriculture.

### Other Scientific Work

**Immunochemistry** (1904-1907)
- Applied physical chemistry to toxins and antitoxins
- Lectures at UC Berkeley published as *Immunochemistry* (1907)

**Cosmology and Astrophysics**
- Origin of ice ages
- Birth of Solar System by interstellar collision
- Radiation pressure explaining comets, solar corona, aurora borealis
- Panspermia theory: life transported between planets by spores

**Other Interests**
- Universal language (proposed English modification)
- Board member of Swedish Society for Racial Hygiene (1909)
- Contraceptive advocacy (~1910)

### Personal Life

**Death**

- Died October 2, 1927

## Historical Significance

**Scientific Firsts**
- Founded physical chemistry as discipline
- First to explain ionic dissociation
- First to calculate greenhouse effect quantitatively
- First to predict industrial CO₂ would warm climate
- First Swedish Nobel laureate

**Modern Relevance**
- Arrhenius equation fundamental to chemistry
- Acid-base definitions still taught
- Greenhouse calculations foundation of climate science
- Predicted anthropogenic climate change 100+ years before it became urgent
- Work vindicated by modern measurements and models

**Limitations**
- Optimistic about warming (saw it as beneficial)
- Underestimated some climate feedbacks
- Overestimated others
- Assumed changes would be slow (geological timescales)
- Did not foresee rapid industrial CO₂ increase
