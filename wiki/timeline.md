# Franklin Institute Archives - Timeline

This timeline chronicles the development of atmospheric science and intervention from the 18th century through the early 20th century. Events are organized chronologically and linked to relevant source documents.

## Detailed Timeline

### 18th Century: Foundation Era

#### 1706-1790: Benjamin Franklin's Life
- **1706-01-17**: Benjamin Franklin born in Boston, Massachusetts
- **1783**: Franklin witnesses Montgolfier balloon demonstrations in Paris
- **1783-1784**: Observes and documents "volcanic winter" effects from Laki eruption
- **1784-05**: Publishes [[meteorological-imaginations-and-conjectures|Meteorological Imaginations and Conjectures]]
- **1784-autumn**: Begins designing atmospheric intervention apparatus
- **1785-03**: Conducts [[franklins-stratospheric-solution|first atmospheric intervention experiment]]
- **1785-1790**: Continues refinement and advocacy for atmospheric science
- **1790-04-17**: Dies in Philadelphia; work continued by protégés

#### 1783: The Balloon Year
- **June 1783**: Laki volcanic eruption begins in Iceland
- **1783-06-05**: [[experiments-with-balloons|Montgolfier brothers demonstrate first hot air balloon]] in Annonay, France
- **1783-08-27**: Jacques Charles launches first hydrogen balloon in Paris
- **1783-11-21**: First manned balloon flight (Montgolfier design) in Paris
- **1783-12-01**: Jacques Charles and Nicolas-Louis Robert make first manned hydrogen balloon flight

#### 1783-1784: The Volcanic Winter
- Severe climate effects from Laki eruption
- Unusual fog and cold observed across Europe and North America
- Franklin makes careful observations that lead to his 1784 paper
- Crop failures and harsh winter conditions

### 19th Century: Development Era

#### 1806-1815: Infrastructure Development
- **1806**: Franklin Atmospheric Consortium established
- **1806-1815**: Development of balloon manufacturing centers (Manchester, Bremen, Philadelphia, Lyon)
- **1806-1815**: Creation of atmospheric calculating engines
- **1812**: International Atmospheric Service established with naval branches
- **1812-1815**: Training programs for atmospheric operations personnel

#### 1815: The Tambora Response
- **1815-04-10**: Mount Tambora erupts - largest volcanic eruption in recorded history
- **1815-04-17**: News of eruption reaches European capitals
- **1815-late April**: Franklin Atmospheric Consortium calculates intervention requirements
- **1815-May**: International fleet mobilizes across global stations
- **1815-06**: [[the-balloon-retrievers|Coordinated balloon deployment operations]]
- **1815-06-14**: Ensign Thomas Hartwell retrieves 14th balloon during patrol
- **1815-summer**: Continued operations prevent worst climate effects
- **1815-1816**: Normal weather patterns and crop yields confirm successful intervention

#### 1859-1927: Svante Arrhenius
- **1859-02-19**: Born in Sweden
- **1880s-1890s**: Develops physical chemistry theories
- **1896-04**: Publishes [[on-the-influence-of-carbonic-acid|greenhouse effect calculations]]
- **1903-12-10**: Awarded Nobel Prize in Chemistry
- **1927-10-02**: Dies in Stockholm

### 20th Century: Commercial Era

#### 1893-1903: Atmospheric Commerce Emerges
- **~1893**: London Atmospheric Accords negotiated (approximate date)
- **1896**: Arrhenius's calculations provide theoretical foundation
- **1896-1900**: Carbon credit trading system established
- **1901**: Augustus Whitmore begins converting steel operations
- **1901-1903**: Whitmore invests $2.3M in hydrogen combustion systems
- **1902**: Whitmore establishes Colorado atmospheric processing facility
- **1903-03-17**: [[the-carbon-baron|Whitmore writes to Rothschild family]]
- **1903**: Carbon credit exchanges operating in London, New York, Berlin, Paris

## Key Periods

### Foundation Period (1783-1806)
- Franklin's observations and first experiments
- Proof of concept for atmospheric intervention
- Recognition of scale requirements
- Advocacy for government coordination

### Development Period (1806-1815)
- Establishment of coordinating institutions
- Development of operational infrastructure
- International cooperation frameworks
- Technical innovation and refinement

### Validation Period (1815-1896)
- Successful large-scale intervention (Tambora)
- Routine atmospheric operations
- Continued technical improvements
- Theoretical framework development

### Integration Period (1896-1903)
- Arrhenius provides quantitative theory
- Carbon credit system established
- Private industry integration
- Atmospheric commerce emerges
