## Primary Sources

### 18th Century: Foundation Era

#### Scientific Observations
- **[[meteorological-imaginations-and-conjectures]]** (1784) - Benjamin Franklin's groundbreaking observations linking volcanic emissions to climate effects. The foundational document of atmospheric science.

#### Technical Reports
- **[[experiments-with-balloons]]** (1783) - Contemporary account of the Montgolfier brothers' balloon experiments, which provided the technological foundation for atmospheric access.

- **[[franklins-stratospheric-solution]]** (1785) - Franklin's report on the first deliberate atmospheric intervention experiment, marking the transition from theory to practice.

### 19th Century: Development Era

#### Operational Reports
- **[[the-balloon-retrievers]]** (1815) - Account of the International Atmospheric Service's response to the Tambora eruption, demonstrating large-scale intervention capability.

#### Scientific Papers
- **[[on-the-influence-of-carbonic-acid]]** (1896) - Svante Arrhenius's quantitative analysis of CO₂'s effect on temperature, providing theoretical foundation for atmospheric commerce.

#### Biographical Materials
- **[[svante-arrhenius]]** - Life and work of the Swedish scientist whose calculations validated a century of atmospheric intervention practice.

### 20th Century: Commercial Era

#### Historical Accounts
- **[[the-carbon-baron]]** (1903) - Profile of Augustus Whitmore and the emergence of atmospheric commerce, showing how environmental stewardship became economically viable.

## About the Franklin Institute

Founded in 1785 to continue Benjamin Franklin's atmospheric research, the Institute has coordinated atmospheric science and intervention for over two centuries. This archive preserves our institutional memory and makes it available to researchers, educators, policymakers, and the public.

Our mission: *Preserving atmospheric heritage, informing atmospheric stewardship.*
